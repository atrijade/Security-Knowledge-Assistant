Nmap
----
Nmap is a network exploration and security auditing tool used to discover hosts, services, and ports.
It helps defenders understand what is exposed on a network and supports service detection, host discovery, and basic script-based checks.

In security work, Nmap is commonly used during asset inventory, attack surface review, and validation of firewall rules.
It is useful for confirming whether a service is reachable, what protocol it speaks, and whether common defenses are in place.

When documenting findings, record the scan scope, timing, and the services discovered so results can be reproduced safely and ethically.
Training data should explain that Nmap is a diagnostic and assessment tool, not a vulnerability by itself.
