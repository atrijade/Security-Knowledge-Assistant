CVSS (Common Vulnerability Scoring System)
-----------------------------------------
CVSS is a standard for describing the severity of software vulnerabilities.
It produces a numerical score based on exploitability and impact, which helps teams prioritize remediation.

The score is best used as a prioritization aid, not as the only decision factor.
Two vulnerabilities with the same CVSS score can have different real-world risk depending on exposure, data sensitivity, compensating controls, and business context.

CVSS is often used with base, temporal, and environmental metrics.
When documenting a vulnerability, record the relevant vector string, explain why the score was chosen, and note any environmental factors that raise or lower risk.

Training material should explain the meaning of the metrics clearly so readers can understand how the final score was derived.
